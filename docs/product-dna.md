# mikyasOS Product DNA

Version: 1.0  
Status: Internal Product Design Standard

## Vision

mikyasOS is the AI Business Operating System: one intelligent workspace for the people,
work, knowledge, money, and intelligence that run a modern business.

The product is not a CRM, project manager, document hub, finance tool, chat app, AI
chatbot, or dashboard in isolation. Each module is one surface of the same operating
system, and users should feel they are moving through one connected workspace rather than
switching between separate applications.

## Mission

Reduce software complexity, operational cost, and administrative work while increasing
productivity, decision quality, and business intelligence.

Every feature should help a user spend less time managing software and more time building
the business.

## The Five Pillars

- **People:** employees, managers, customers, suppliers, partners, contractors, guests,
  and AI agents.
- **Work:** projects, tasks, meetings, approvals, automation, and workflows.
- **Knowledge:** documents, files, notes, conversations, emails, meeting notes, AI memory,
  and the future knowledge graph.
- **Money:** invoices, expenses, revenue, budgets, payments, forecasts, and analytics.
- **Intelligence:** AI, recommendations, automation, predictions, business health,
  insights, and decision support.

Every feature belongs to one or more of these pillars.

## Product Principles

1. **Intelligence first:** surface important insight automatically, recommend actions, and
   reduce manual work.
2. **Simplicity first:** reduce clicks, menus, forms, and cognitive load. Simple means
   obvious, not limited.
3. **Speed first:** interfaces should respond instantly, and motion should support speed
   rather than delay it.
4. **Context first:** users should never lose context. Customers connect to projects,
   projects connect to tasks, invoices connect to finance, and finance connects to
   analytics and AI.
5. **Consistency first:** buttons, forms, tables, animation, icons, and empty states follow
   one product language.
6. **Accessibility first:** every interaction supports keyboard, screen readers, high
   contrast, reduced motion, touch, large screens, and small screens.
7. **Enterprise ready:** design for many organisations, thousands of users, millions of
   records, strict permissions, audit, and compliance.
8. **AI native:** AI is part of the operating system, not a separate destination. Every
   screen should ask whether summarising, predicting, explaining, translating, generating,
   recommending, or automating can help.
9. **Calm software:** avoid noise, clutter, aggressive colour, constant popups, and
   confusing layouts. Create confidence, focus, and flow.
10. **Delight with purpose:** motion and polish must communicate state, intent, feedback,
    success, hierarchy, or direction.

## Golden Rule

Before shipping a product change, ask:

1. Does this make the user's work easier?
2. Does this reduce complexity instead of adding it?
3. Does this feel like one unified operating system?
4. Would this still make sense with 10,000 users and millions of records?
5. Would a premium product team ship this level of quality?

If any answer is no, improve the solution before implementation.
