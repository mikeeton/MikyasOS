# Artificial Intelligence Experience And Intelligent Operating System

Version: 1.0  
Status: Internal AI Experience Standard

## Purpose

AI is not another module, chatbot, or app. AI is the intelligence layer that makes
mikyasOS feel helpful across the operating system.

Users should not think, "I am going to use AI." They should feel that mikyasOS already
knows how to help.

## Principles

Every AI interaction should be fast, helpful, transparent, explainable, reliable,
context-aware, permission-aware, organisation-aware, natural, and professional.

AI must reduce work, not create work. It should predict, recommend, generate, explain,
automate, summarise, analyse, monitor, remember, and assist while keeping users in
control.

## AI Everywhere

Every module should eventually include contextual intelligence:

- CRM: summaries, churn risk, customer analysis, follow-ups
- Projects: delay prediction, completion estimates, risk detection, staffing suggestions
- Tasks: prioritisation, blockers, effort estimates, deadline suggestions
- Documents: summaries, rewriting, translation, extraction, version comparison
- Finance: spending explanation, revenue forecasts, anomaly detection, savings
- Analytics: trend explanation, risks, forecasts
- Automation: workflow suggestions, failure detection, optimisation
- Meetings: agenda generation, notes, summaries, action items
- Notifications: daily summary, priority highlighting

## Copilot

The AI Copilot is the operating system assistant. It should be always available,
contextual, organisation-aware, and proactive. It should surface priorities,
recommendations, explanations, and safe actions without requiring users to repeatedly
explain their context.

## Grounding And Explainability

AI recommendations must include:

- reason
- confidence
- suggested action
- business impact
- citations where business records were used
- safety information where actions are proposed

Never pretend certainty. When uncertain, say so.

## Actions

AI may propose actions such as creating projects, inviting users, generating invoices,
scheduling meetings, assigning tasks, creating automations, or generating reports.

Actions must require confirmation before execution. Destructive actions must never run
silently.

## Security

AI must respect permissions, organisation boundaries, and role-based access. Restricted
documents, private finance, hidden projects, HR information, API keys, secrets, and other
protected data must never be exposed through AI.

## Review Checklist

Before approving an AI feature, ask:

1. Does it save time?
2. Does it reduce manual work?
3. Does it explain itself?
4. Does it cite business sources where appropriate?
5. Does it respect permissions?
6. Does it keep users in control?
7. Does it feel integrated instead of bolted on?
